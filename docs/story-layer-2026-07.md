## Why

The activities work, but they are arcade machines floating in the void.
Nobody owns them. The fix is not more mechanics — it is **purpose**: every
playable thing exists because someone here needs it. That someone is a Belter
drift-colony, and the inspiration is The Expanse — *inspired*, never copied:
our own names, the show's texture (Belters, ice haulers, raiders, slingshot
racing), nothing that requires having watched it.

## The premise, in one breath

This corner of space is a working Belt neighborhood. The seven bright systems
are the ports — the "official" content. In the seams, Belters live on a
drift-colony dug into a rock in the Projects belt. Everything playable is a
**standing job** in their neighborhood.

## Canon anchors (researched)

- **Slingshot racing is Expanse canon**: an illegal Belter sport — homemade
  craft, plotted courses, "vying for the fast time across an established
  circuit" (Manéo Jung-Espinoza of Ceres, his salvage-built *Y Que*, S3
  "Delta-V"). The Belt Run IS this sport; we hand it to its rightful owners.
- **Ice haulers** are the Belt's lifeline (the *Canterbury* archetype); the
  wreck of one is the saddest object in the genre.
- **Lang Belta** (Nick Farmer's creole): appears ONLY as painted signage in
  the world — set dressing, like foreign-language signs in any game city.

## The situation (not a plot)

Four things are TRUE AT ONCE, and ongoing. No order, no chain, no
conclusion — a working neighborhood has recurring jobs, not a campaign.
Repeats are diegetic by construction: another drill, another heat, another
skiff, another convoy.

- **THE WRECK** *(static history — build S2)*: raiders hit the ice hauler
  **Nilak** on approach, months back. Her hull drifts near the colony with
  a painted beacon plaque: `REMEMBER THE NILAK`. Nothing to play — it is
  the reason the militia exists, and it fulfills the old README derelict
  promise.
- **THE DRILL** *(live today — Gunnery Range, reframed)*: the colony militia's
  continuous readiness program. Marquee: `DRIFT MILITIA · PDC CERTIFICATION ·
  LIVE FIRE`. A certification run is repeatable by definition.
- **THE HUNT** *(planned F.3)*: raider skiffs still prowl the corridor; the
  militia keeps a standing interdiction posting. There is always another
  skiff on the scope.
- **THE ICE RUN** *(planned F.4)*: haulers run ice to port on a schedule;
  escort is a standing contract. There is always a next convoy.

And the culture beat:

- **THE CIRCUIT** *(live today — Belt Run, reframed)*: the colony's slingshot
  circuit. The drift kids strung the gates; the racing club keeps the board.
  Marquee: `DRIFT RACING CLUB · SLINGSHOT CIRCUIT`.

Explorers who see everything will connect the wreck to the militia to the
raiders to the escorts themselves. That click is the reward — never a quest
log entry.

## Non-watcher guarantee

Everything must read in three seconds with zero lore: *militia range*,
*racing club*, *memorial wreck*, *mining colony*, *escort contract* — frontier
logic, universally legible. The Expanse-ness lives only in texture (welded
Belter aesthetic, names, creole signage) that watchers recognize and everyone
else experiences as "a lived-in place with its own dialect." **The HUD speaks
plain English, always.**

## How story reaches the visitor — three channels, all existing

1. **The place itself**: the settlement, the wreck, gates that visibly belong
   to the colony. Environmental storytelling does the heavy lifting.
2. **Names**: marquee sub-lines, POI labels, tactical-MFD detail lines
   rewritten diegetically. Zero new UI surfaces.
3. **Painted signage**: the Nilak plaque, the colony welcome board
   (`OYE — INTERAMNIA DRIFT` + English beneath), the racing club's crooked
   best-times board near the START gate.

**Hard rule**: if a line of flavor cannot be painted on a world surface or
fit in a label that already exists, it does not ship. No hail popups, no
named characters addressing the player, no cutscenes, no portraits.

## The place: the drift-colony

- **Name (LOCKED)**: **704 Interamnia → "Interamnia Drift"**, clipped by
  locals to "the Amnia" — real minor planet the show never spotlighted (the
  show's own naming logic: Ceres, Eros, Pallas are real rocks).
- **Where**: inside the Projects belt arc, ~500u off the Belt Run course.
- **Build** (assets-first; verified downloadable CC-BY candidates): hollowed
  rock core ("Asteroid with Internal Tunnel System", 7.8k faces) + industrial
  structure ("Space Station Asteroid Mining Facility" / "Space Colony Modular
  Kit Bash", optimized through the offline pipeline like the Gateway
  45.8→6.6MB) + our nav buoys, floodlights, window emissives, slow spin.
  Welded, patched, working — not shiny.
- **Not a content system**: no resume boards. The Comms Station remains the
  professional front door.

## What stays sacred

- No meta-progression: no quest log, reputation, badges, unlock chains.
- Friction is the fun: story explains mechanics, never adds them.
- Realism bar: acquired assets through the offline pipeline; functional UI.
- Finite wins per run; the WORLD is what persists, not player state.
